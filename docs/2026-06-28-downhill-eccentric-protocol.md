# Downhill / Eccentric Protocol — Injury Prevention for the Descents

**Why:** Technical Copper Canyon descents are where quads blow up late in a 59 km race. A single hard
downhill bout causes severe quad damage (force −16%, explosive power −37%, 3–4 days to recover) — but
**trained** downhill legs adapt (the "repeated-bout effect") and *retain* strength while untrained ones
collapse. This is the highest-leverage thing you can do to protect your knees/quads and finish strong.

## The weekly downhill session (1×/week, build weeks 20–26)

1. Easy warm-up 10–15 min on flat/rolling.
2. **Downhill reps** on a moderate grade (~5–12%), smooth surface to start:
   - Start: **4 × 90 s** controlled downhill, easy jog/walk back up to recover.
   - Build over the weeks to **6–8 × 90–120 s**.
   - Form: quick, light, *quiet* steps; slight forward lean from the ankles; don't brake hard with the heels — let gravity do it under control.
3. Easy cool-down 10 min.

Effort is **easy–moderate**, not a sprint. The goal is *eccentric exposure*, not speed.

## Progression
| Weeks | Reps × duration | Grade/terrain |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| 20–22 | 4–5 × 90 s | moderate, smooth |
| 23–24 | 6 × 90–120 s | moderate, add some uneven ground |
| 26 | 6–8 × 120 s | steeper / more technical |
| 21, 25 (recovery) & 28–30 (taper) | **skip** | — |

## Also (free downhill volume)
- Run your **long runs and hill sessions on hilly/technical terrain** — descend relaxed and controlled. This is descent practice that doubles as the session you're already doing.

## Cautions (this is high-impact)
- **Introduce gradually.** The first 1–2 sessions will make your quads sore (DOMS) — that's the adaptation; it fades fast with repetition.
- **Don't** do downhill reps the day *before* a long run or on tired/under-recovered days.
- Stop if you feel **knee joint** pain (not just muscle soreness).
- Pair with the Thursday strength work (squats/step-downs) — same protective adaptation.

## In your plan
Weeks **20 and 24** are tagged `downhill` as the explicit dedicated sessions; otherwise fold controlled
descents into your hill days and long runs.
