# Gut-Training & Race-Fueling Protocol — UTT 59 km

**Start:** now (week 18) · **Race:** Oct 2 2026 · **Goal:** tolerate 80–90 g carb/h to finish strong
**Evidence:** events >2h → 60–90 g/h; >60 g/h needs **2:1 glucose:fructose** (dual transporters ↑ oxidation ~50%); gut adapts over 6–10 wks of progressive practice. (Jeukendrup/ACSM/ISSN; Viribay 2020; Costa et al.)

## Why
The gut is trainable. Most DNFs/blow-ups in long trail races are fueling failures, not fitness. You have **~14 weeks** — enough to build tolerance from a comfortable baseline to race target, *if you start now*.

## The carb-per-hour ramp (practice on the long run each week)

| Wk | Long run | Carb target (g/h) | Notes |
|----|----------|-------------------|-------|
| 18 | 10 km | **50** | Establish comfortable baseline; note any GI distress |
| 19 | 12 km | 50 | Same — lock in tolerance |
| 20 | 15 km | **60** | First step up; from here use 2:1 glucose:fructose |
| 21 | 10 km (rec) | 60 | Easy week — keep practicing, don't push |
| 22 | 16 km (B2B) | **70** | Fuel **both** back-to-back days |
| 23 | 20 km | 70 | Longer duration at 70 |
| 24 | 24 km (B2B) | **80** | Race-target zone; fuel both days |
| 25 | 15 km (rec) | 70 | Dial back slightly on recovery week |
| 26 | 28 km (B2B) | **85** | Near race rate, long duration |
| 27 | 32 km | **90** | **Full dress rehearsal** — exact race products, kit, timing |
| 28–30 | taper | 60–80 | Maintain gut familiarity; don't introduce anything new |

## How to execute each long run
- **Start fueling early** (within the first 30–45 min), not when you're already depleted.
- Spread intake across the hour (e.g., every 15–20 min), don't dump it.
- **Above 60 g/h: use a 2:1 glucose:fructose source** (most modern gels/drink mixes/chews are formulated this way — check the label, e.g. Maurten, SiS Beta Fuel, Precision Fuel). Below 60 g/h, total amount matters more than the ratio.
- Pair carbs with adequate fluid; at altitude add 500–1000 ml extra hydration (per your coach knowledge base).
- **Log GI response** (Garmin note / training log): bloating, nausea, cramping → hold the current level another week before stepping up.

## Race day (Oct 2)
- Target **80–90 g/h** of the *exact* products you rehearsed in weeks 24–27. **Nothing new on race day.**
- For a ~6–7 h finish that's roughly **500–630 g total carb** — plan your carry + aid-station resupply around it.
- 120 g/h is unnecessary for this goal and a higher GI-risk; 80–90 is the sweet spot.
