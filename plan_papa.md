## Weekly rhythm (5 days)

- **Mon / Fri:** rest + 10-min home prehab (single-leg step-downs, calf raises, hip/glute)
- **Tue / Thu:** easy aerobic runs (~125 bpm, conversational)
- **Wed:** descent / vert session — downhill repeats (the #1 specific work)
- **Sat:** long run (shared with Emmanuel) — time-on-feet + climbing
- **Sun:** short easy second run (skip if tired on recovery weeks)

## Injury guardrails (top priority)

- **Ramp cap** — no weekly volume jump >~10% between build weeks; back off if it feels hard.
- **Recovery weeks 2 & 6 are non-negotiable** — drop ~30%, synced with Emmanuel's recovery weeks.
- **All easy runs truly easy** — hold ~125 bpm. Almost no hard intensity by design.
- **Descent progression is gradual** — short controlled descents early; let the repeated-bout effect build quad durability.
- **Home prehab 2×/week** — 10 min, biggest injury-prevention ROI without a gym.
- **Pain rules** — sharp/joint pain: stop and rest/swap. Any niggle >3 days: cut volume until it settles. Never train through joint pain.
- **Peak long run capped at 37 km** (< his proven 38 km), run-hike easy.
- **Heat prep in the final ~3 weeks** — altitude is already covered by his highland training; canyon heat is the race-day wildcard.

## The catch-up

Your long runs both sit on Saturday, same trail. He's fitter with a bigger base, so he runs longer — but the gap closes through the build. **Aug 31–Sep 6 (Week 8): your peak long run of 32 km matches his — you run 32 km side by side.** Then he does one solo 37 km peak while you taper, and on **Oct 2 you run the 59 km together.**
